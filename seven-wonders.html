Country : Italy

Founded : AD 70 - 80

Used For : This utilized a great variety of wild beasts, mainly imported from Africa and the Middle East, and included creatures such as rhinoceros, hippopotamuses, elephants, giraffes, aurochs, wisents, Barbary lions, panthers, leopards, bears, Caspian tigers, crocodiles and ostriches. Battles and hunts were often staged amid elaborate sets with movable trees and buildings.

The word Colosseum is a neuter Latin noun formed from the adjective colosseus, meaning "gigantic". Colosseo is an elliptical amphitheatre in the centre of the city of Rome, Italy and it is just east of the Roman Forum. It is the largest ancient amphitheatre ever built, and is still the largest standing amphitheatre in the world, despite its age.

The three emperors who were patrons of the work are known as the Flavian dynasty, and the amphitheatre was named the Flavian Amphitheatre by later by later classicists and archaeologists for its association with their family name (Flavius).

Reason for Famous: The Colosseum of Italy is famous for the scene of thousands of hand-to-hand combats between gladiators, of contests between men and animals, and of many larger combats, including mock naval engagements.

Details

Country : Jordan

Founded : BC 3rd century

Religious Importance : The Nabataeans worshipped Arab gods & goddesses during the pre-Islamic era with few of their deified kings. Obodas was deified after his death. Dushara was the primary male god accompanied by his 3 female deities: Al-'Uzzā, Allat & Manāt. Many statues carved in the rock depict these gods & goddesses. New evidence indicates that broader Edomite, & Nabataean theology had strong links to Earth-Sun relationships, often manifested in the orientation of prominent Petra structures to equinox & solstice sunrises & sunsets.

Petra is a historic and archaeological city in southern Jordan. It is also called the "Rose City" because of the colour of the sandstone from which it is carved. it was famously called "a rose-red city half as old as time" in a poem of 1845 by John Burgon.

It is adjacent to the mountain of Jabal Al-Madbah, in a basin surrounded by mountains forming the eastern flank of the Arabah valley running from the Dead Sea to the Gulf of Aqaba. Most of the famous rock-cut buildings, which are mainly tombs, date from this and the following period.

Reason for Famous: The Outstanding Universal Value of Petra resides in the vast extent of elaborate tomb and temple architecture, religious high places. The tunnels and diversion dams that combined with a vast network of cisterns and reservoirs which controlled and conserved seasonal rains.

Chichén Itzá

Know More...

Details

Country : Mexico

Founded : AD 600-750

Used for : The Great Ball Court about 150 meters to the north-west of the Castillo is the most impressive. It is the largest and best preserved ball court in ancient Mesoamerica. It measures 168 by 70 meters. The parallel platforms flanking the main playing area are each 95 meters (312 ft) long.

Chichén Itzá was one of the largest Maya cities and it was likely to have been one of the mythical great cities, or Tollans, referred to in later Mesoamerican literature. The city may have had the most diverse population in the Maya world, a factor that could have contributed to the variety of architectural styles at the site.

Traditional Yucatec Maya spelling in Latin letters, used from 16th - mid 20th century, spelled it as "Chichen Itza". Late Classic city was centered upon the area to southwest of the Xtoloc cenote, with the main architecture represented by the substructures now underlying the Las Monjas & Observatorio & the platform which they built.

Reason for Famous: As one of the largest and best-preserved archaeological sites in the world, Chichen Itza is home to a myriad of ancient Mayan marvels and rich cultural histories cherished by travelers across the globe.

Christ the Redeemer, is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, created by French-Polish sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot. Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida sculpted the face.

Machu Picchu

Know More...

Details

Country : Peru

Founded : AD 1450

Used for : Much of the farming done at Machu Picchu was done on its hundreds of man-made terraces. These terraces were a work of considerable engineering, built to ensure good drainage and soil fertility while also protecting the mountain itself from erosion and landslides. As studies of the land show that there were landslides that happened during the construction of Machu Picchu.

Machu Picchu is referred to as the "Lost City of the Incas", it is the most familiar icon of the Inca Empire. It is located in the Machupicchu District within Urubamba Province above the Sacred Valley, which is 80 kilometers (50 miles) northwest of Cusco. The Urubamba River flows past it, cutting through the Cordillera and creating a canyon with a tropical mountain climate.

The names of the buildings, their supposed uses, and their inhabitants, are the product of modern archaeologists based on physical evidence, including tombs at the site. Its three primary structures are the Intihuatana, the Temple of the Sun, & the Temple of the Three Windows.

Reason for Famous: More than 7,000 feet above sea level in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu is the most visited tourist destination in Peru. A symbol of the Incan Empire and built around 1450 AD.

Details

Country : India

Founded : 1631 - 1648

Inspiration : The imperial court documenting Shah Jahan's grief after the death of Mumtaz Mahal illustrates the love story held as the inspiration for the Taj Mahal. He did not show the same level of affection to others as he had shown her while she was alive. He avoided royal affairs for a week due to his grief, along with giving up listening to music and dressing lavishly for two years.

Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1631 by the fifth Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan (1628 - 1658) to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

"The jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage". It is regarded by many as the best example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of India's rich history. Various types of symbolism have been employed in the Taj to reflect natural beauty and divinity.

Reason for Famous: The Taj Mahal is famous for its stunning beauty and grandeur. The monument is a magnificent white marble mausoleum, which was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Great Wall of China

Know More...

Details

Country : China

Founded : BC 220

Used for : The purpose have included border controls, allowing the imposition of duties on goods transported along the Silk Road, regulation or encouragement of trade & the control of immigration and emigration. The defensive characteristics were enhanced by the construction of watchtowers, troop barracks, garrison stations, signaling capabilities through the means of smoke or fire, & the fact that the path of the Great Wall also served as a transportation corridor.

The Great Wall of China literally "ten thousand li long wall" is a series of fortifications that were built across the historical northern borders of ancient Chinese states and Imperial China as protection against various nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppe. Several walls were built from as early as the 7th century BC, with selective stretches later joined by Qin Shi Huang the first emperor of China.

Many successive dynasties built and maintained multiple stretches of border walls. The best-known sections of the wall were built by the Ming dynasty. The defensive system of the Great Wall is generally recognized as one of the most impressive architectural feats in history.

Reason for Famous: It is a massive construction size, and its unique architectural style as well. A great army of manpower, composed of soldiers, prisoners, and local people, built the wall. To serve as defense against enemy attack from nomadic tribes north of China.
