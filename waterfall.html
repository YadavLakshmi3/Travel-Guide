A waterfall is any point in a river or stream where water flows over a vertical drop or a series of steep drops.

Waterfalls are often visited by people simply to see them. Activities at waterfalls can include Swimming, Photography, Fishing, generating Hydroelectric Power, Rafting, etc...

We have a distinct relationship with waterfalls for years, travelling to See them, Explore and Name them.

Waterfalls can be formed in several ways, but the most common method of formation is that a river courses over a top layer of resistant bedrock before falling on to softer rock, which erodes faster, leading to an increasingly high fall.

A waterfall is generally defined as a point in a river where water flows over a steep drop that is close to or directly vertical. Waterfalls are commonly formed in the upper course of a river where lakes flow into valleys in steep mountains.

When warm and cold water meet by a gorge in the ocean, large underwater waterfalls can form as the cold water rushes to the bottom.
