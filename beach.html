A Beach is a narrow strip of land separating a body of water from inland areas. Beaches are usually made of sand, tiny grains of rocks and minerals that have been worn down by constant pounding by wind and waves.

The particles of a beach are sometimes biological in origin, such as mollusc shells or bits of coral and sometimes bits of igneous rock, but the most common mineral in beaches is quartz.

Beaches are never static. They are always being built up or more quickly than other landforms. Over time the boundary between the land and the sea changes. It usually consists of loose particles, such as sand, gravel, shingle, or pebbles.

People often use beaches for recreation. They swim, bask in the sun, or just relax. Beaches are also used for diving or for seeing marine life.

Beaches are natural landing and launching places for boats, and landing craft are specially made for beaches.

A beach is a landform along the coast of an ocean, sea, lake, or river. The most popular beaches have fine white or light-colored sand and warm water to swim in.
