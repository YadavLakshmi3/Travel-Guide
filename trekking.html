Trekking, the meaning of the term originated in South Africa in the nineteenth century, after the English conquest of the territory.

The Dutch, defeated, had to leave and cross thorny paths, hence the word Trekking. In 1965 Colonel Jimmy Roberts introduced the trekking to the world.

It is traveling on foot in wilderness or countryside. Day hikers return before nightfall. This is an outdoor activity of walking for more than a day. It is a form of walking, undertaken with the specific purpose of exploring and enjoying the scenery.

This could be a motive, It could be a commitment, an aim, an objective, a mission, a party, a social gathering. People usually carry water, food, a map, hat, and rain-proof gear.

The word "Trekking" describes all forms of walking, whether it is a walk in the park or backpacking in the heaps or hill paths.

Trekking to Religious Pilgrimages have existed much longer but they involve walking long distances for a spiritual purpose associated with specific religions.
